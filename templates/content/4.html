What is the decision?

Your family member is still on a ventilator. Despite the best care from the doctors and nurses, he or she is unable to survive without the ventilator. There are a number of reasons for this, for example:

  • Your family member is unconscious because of the brain injury, and the breathing tube is protecting the lungs from saliva or food going down the windpipe;
  • Your family member has problems with the lungs themselves (lung infection, fluid on the lungs, or other condition);
  • Your family member has other issues, which the doctor can explain.
At this point, you have to decide which of two treatment goals best aligns with your family memberâ€™s goals, values, and preferences.
The treatment goal that you choose will determine the procedures and type of care your family member receives while in the ICU and afterwards.

What are the two options?

Option 1
Survival, no matter what it takes

Option 2
As comfortable as possible
So he or she does not suffer

Both options are explained in more detail over the next few pages.
