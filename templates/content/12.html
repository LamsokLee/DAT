Comparing and summarizing different treatment options

Reasons for choosing this option Reasons for avoiding this option

“Do everything”


Focus on survival
  • It gives the patient the best chance of staying alive, even if just for a while.
  • The family believes that the patient will have a quality of life that he or she finds acceptable.
  • The patient has said in the past that he or she wants to be kept alive at all costs.
  • The patient would feel OK with being uncomfortable from lying in bed or working hard during therapy sessions.
  • Additional treatments can be painful, or make the patient uncomfortable
  • The patient has said in the past that he or she does not wish to be dependent on others to live.
  • The family knows that the patient does not want a trach or feeding tube, even if just temporarily.
  • The patient may still die, even with advanced treatments, and will suffer as a result of those measures.

“No suffering”


Focus on comfort
  • It is unlikely that the patient will have a quality of life that is acceptable to him or her.
  • Family members believe that the patient has suffered enough; they want the patient to be comfortable and at peace.
  • Without life support, the patient will die.
  • The patient would choose to undergo advanced treatments and live in a nursing home to survive if able to speak on the matter.
