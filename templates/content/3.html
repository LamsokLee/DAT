What is a mechanical ventilator?

    A mechanical ventilator is a breathing machine that provides artificial breathing support. The ventilator pumps air and oxygen into the lungs through a breathing tube. The breathing tube goes through the mouth into the windpipe. The ventilator keeps a patient breathing while he or she receives treatments for medical problems like TBI and other serious conditions.
    It is also called “life support.” The ventilator itself does not cure the injury.

What is it like to be on a ventilator?

Patients are very sick, and:

  • Are uncomfortable
  • Often need medicines for pain and to help them relax.
  • Cannot eat or drink while because of the breathing tube in their mouth.
  • Cannot speak or make a sound.
Many patients with brain injury cannot breathe or swallow on their own for weeks or months. Soon, the doctor will ask you to make an important decision regarding the ventilator.
