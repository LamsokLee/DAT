What is your family member’s prognosis?

Many families want to know about their family member’s outlook (“prognosis”) and the possible long-term effects of TBI before making a decision on treatment.

It is important to note that:
  • There is a wide range of possible long-term effects after TBI
  • It is rare for doctors to be able to tell you for sure what the prognosis is
  • In most cases, there is a large degree of uncertainty

this is because the prognosis depends on many factors, including:

  • Severity of the brain injury when it happened
  • Age of the person at the time of the injury
  • Health of the person before the brain injury
  • Status of the person’s memory and thinking ability before the brain injury
  • Blood pressure and oxygen levels of the person at the time of and right after the brain injury
  • Bleeding in the brain
  • Injuries to other organs (lung, belly, etc.)
  • Other complications in the icu (brain swelling, infections, etc.)
  • Ability and willingness of the person to overcome difficult times
  • Other unknown factors
