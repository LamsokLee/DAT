IF I CHOOSE SURVIVAL AS THE GOAL OF CARE, WHAT HAPPENS WITH MY LOVED ONE AFTER THEY LEAVE THE ICU?


After the trach and feeding tube placement, the doctor decides when your loved one is ready to leave the ICU. They need additional care to get better, which is not provided in the ICU. They will not be able to go home because they need further medical care and rehabilitation. There are different types of places specialized in caring for people recovering from TBI. Different places provide different levels of care. Your loved one’s medical and nursing needs determine which type of place they go to after the ICU:

Acute Rehabilitation Hospital

Very few serious TBI patients go here directly. Patients who go here are generally very stable and wide awake. They must be able to take part in at least 3 hours of therapy per day for 5-7 days per week. It is focused on providing intense physical, occupational, and speech therapies. Patients who still require a breathing machine, or frequent suctioning through the trach, or are too sleepy, cannot go here until they get better. Patients are treated here for several weeks.

Long-Term Acute Care Hospital (LTACH)

This place is able to provide more complex medical care than an acute rehabilitation hospital. Patients are sicker and dependent on help, and for this reason therapy sessions are usually fewer and less intense. Those patients who get better can be transferred to an acute rehabilitation hospital later.

Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF)

This place is different from a hospital and patients are usually quite stable but need full nursing care due to the effects from their injuries. Patients may be here for a long period of time, or even the rest of their lives. Patients are too ill to take part in several hours of therapy per day. Transition to LTACH or acute rehabilitation hospital is possible, but less likely due to the patient’s high level of sickness and dependence on others.
