  • Even after thinking about the decision and the options, you may still not be sure what is best for your family member.
  • It’s ok to feel that way. Many people in this situation feel the same way.
  • You may be unsure because:
    • You need to talk with others before making a decision.
    • You still have questions about treatment choices.
    • You need more time to think about things.

WHERE CAN YOU GET MORE INFORMATION?

It is very important to discuss your decision with your family member’s doctor and get answers to your questions.

  • Many staff members are ready to help and support you, including:
    • doctors,
    • nurses,
    • palliative care specialists,
    • chaplains,
    • social workers, and
    • case managers.
  • You can get more help by speaking to these staff members and meeting with your family member’s doctor.
