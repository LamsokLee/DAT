WHEN COMFORT IS THE GOAL OF CARE


Why choose comfort as the treatment goal (“comfort care”)?

  • The family member has gone through enough discomfort in the ICU at this point.
  • The person’s brain injury is too severe to allow any chance of survival.
  • The person has said in the past that he or she would not want to endure the treatments necessary to be kept alive.
  • The person has made it clear in the past that he or she would not want the lower quality of life to be expected after the TBI.

What happens when comfort is the treatment goal (“comfort care”)?

  • When the family is ready, the bedside team will safely and carefully discontinue medications, except those that prevent suffering.
  • Nurses experienced in providing comfort care will make sure your family member is not suffering.
  • The expert staff will discontinue IV fluids, artificial feeding, breathing tube, and ventilator—measures which are keeping your family member alive.
  • Staff will administer medicines and nursing care to prevent pain and suffering, but they will do nothing to hasten the natural dying process.
  • The patient will die, usually within hours to days after starting comfort care. Family members and friends can stay with the patient during this time.
