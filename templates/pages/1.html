INTRODUCTION


  • Your family member has a serious traumatic brain injury (TBI) and has been in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) for several days.
  • Your family member can’t make decisions about his or her medical care.
  • The doctor needs to know soon the goals that should be considered when treating your family member.
  • Your family member needs your help in deciding which goals to pursue during treatment. This is known as the “goals of care” decision.
  • This is one of the most important decisions to be made in your family member’s medical care.

THIS DECISION AID IS DESIGNED TO HELP YOU


  • Making a decision about your family member’s “goals of care” is difficult for most families; that’s normal.
  • For that reason, we have created this tool (decision aid) to help you.
  • It will help you understand what is involved in the decision and the choices available to you, so that you can make the best decision for your family member.
  • This decision aid was created with input from people like you who had to make this decision for their loved ones.
  • Remember: this tool will not replace a meeting with the doctor.
  • This tool will help you when you talk to the doctor about the decision facing you and the choices available.

  1. Completing this decision aid will take about 30 minutes. You can take breaks if needed.
  2. Please read every page.
  3. Please fill out the worksheet (page 17). This will help you when you talk with the doctor.
  4. Bring the completed worksheet to the meeting with the doctor.
