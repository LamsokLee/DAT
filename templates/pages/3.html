PART 1: THE FACTS



WHAT IS A MECHANICAL VENTILATOR?


  • A mechanical ventilator is a breathing machine that provides artificial breathing support. It is also called “life support.”
  • The ventilator pumps air and oxygen into the lungs through a breathing tube.
  • The breathing tube goes through the mouth into the windpipe.
  • The ventilator keeps a patient breathing while he or she receives treatments for medical problems like TBI and other serious conditions.
    The ventilator itself does not cure the injury.

WHAT IS IT LIKE TO BE ON A VENTILATOR?


  • Patients are uncomfortable when they are on a ventilator.
  • Patients often need medicines for pain and to help them relax while they are on a ventilator.
  • Patients cannot eat or drink while on a ventilator because of the breathing tube in their mouth.
  • Patients cannot speak or make a sound when on a ventilator.
  • Patients who are on a ventilator are very sick.

  • Many TBI patients cannot breathe or swallow on their own for weeks or months. Soon, the doctor will ask you to make an important decision regarding the ventilator.
