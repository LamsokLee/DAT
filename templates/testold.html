Very few serious TBI patients go here directly. Patients who go here are generally very stable and wide awake. They must be able to take part in at least 3 hours of therapy per day for 5-7 days per week. It is focused on providing intense physical, occupational, and speech therapies. Patients who still require a breathing machine, or frequent suctioning through the trach, or are too sleepy, cannot go here until they get better. Patients are treated here for several weeks.

{% if session['accordion4'] == True %} {% else %} {% endif %} Long-Term Acute Care Hospital (LTACH)

This place is able to provide more complex medical care than an acute rehabilitation hospital. Patients are sicker and dependent on help, and for this reason therapy sessions are usually fewer and less intense. Those patients who get better can be transferred to an acute rehabilitation hospital later.

{% if session['accordion5'] == True %} {% else %} {% endif %} Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF)

This place is different from a hospital and patients are usually quite stable but need full nursing care due to the effects from their injuries. Patients may be here for a long period of time, or even the rest of their lives. Patients are too ill to take part in several hours of therapy per day.
