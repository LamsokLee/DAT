WHAT DECISION AM I BEING ASKED TO MAKE?

Your loved one is still on a ventilator, and despite the best care from the doctors and nurses, your loved one is unable to come off the ventilator and breathe on his/her own.

At this point, there are 2 different treatment goals.

You are being asked to make a decision: which of these 2 treatment goals best aligns with your loved one’s goals, values and preferences.

The treatment goal that you choose will affect what kinds of procedures and care are done for your loved one while they are in the ICU and afterwards.

WHAT IS MOST IMPORTANT FOR YOUR LOVED ONE RIGHT NOW?

What do you think is most important treatment for your loved one right now? What is most important to you? Write it down.

Examples: “not to suffer”, “to make it out of the hospital”, “to be able to talk to family”, “to be back to normal like before”, “to go back to work”, “have no pain”, etc.
